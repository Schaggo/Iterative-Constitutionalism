# Iterative-Constitutionalism
*(Working title – [Debatable])*  

An open, collaborative proposal for a **classical liberal governance system** that radically minimizes institutional corruption, elite capture, unaccountability, and concentrated opaque power—while preserving individual liberty, property rights, rule of law, and limited government.

**Status**: Early conceptual draft – invitation to critique, fork, improve, simulate, or reject any part.

## Core Philosophy  
Government exists **only** to protect life, liberty, and property (negative liberties), adjudicate disputes fairly, and ensure equal freedom of participation.  
Everything else is out of scope by default → prevents mission creep and capture points. **[Debatable]** – scope of “protection” (e.g. environmental externalities, public goods)?  

Inspired by:  
- Classical liberalism (Locke, Madison, Hayek)  
- Evidence-based / iterative policymaking (Rules as Code, Government as Platform)  
- Anti-corruption best practices (Hong Kong ICAC, Singapore CPIB, sortition experiments)  

Goal: A system that functions reasonably well **even with widespread public disinterest** in economics/governance, while making corruption personally costly and detectable.

## 1. Core Government Functions (Minimal Viable State)  
- Protection of life and property (military, police, courts)  
- Adjudication of disputes with aim of equal liberty / non-coercion  
- **No** default positive entitlements or broad economic management  

**[Debatable]** – Should emergency public goods (pandemics, basic infrastructure) be allowed via supermajority + sunset?

## 2. Legislative & Regulatory Process (Iterative & Evidence-Driven)  
- Every law/regulation **limited to 1 year** → auto-expires unless explicitly re-enacted **[Debatable]** – 1 vs 2–5 years for stability? Staggered expirations?  
- **Anyone** can propose a law if they provide:  
  - Clear, measurable desired outcomes  
  - Motivation / first-principles reasoning  
  - Predicted impacts (simulations encouraged)  
- Mandatory public discussion period before vote **[Debatable]** – length? AI-assisted summarization?  
- Laws must include falsifiable success metrics → independent audit after implementation  

## 3. Personal Accountability for Policy Failure (“Skin in the Game”)  
- If a law demonstrably **backfires** (worsens target metric by X% after audit), proposers face **direct power reduction**:  
  - Ineligibility for re-election (1–2 terms)  
  - Financial penalties / clawbacks (portion of salary/perks)  
  - Public “failure registry” entry  
- Same for fiscal failures: unbalanced budgets or excessive inflation → 2-period ban **[Debatable]** – exact thresholds? Emergency exemptions? Who defines “excessive”?  

## 4. Anti-Corruption Firewall (Core Focus)  
### a. Radical Financial Transparency  
- Real-time public disclosure of assets, income, debts, transactions (politicians + senior officials + families)  
- Beneficial ownership registers → no anonymous shells  
- Anonymous political donations banned **[Debatable]** – free speech vs. corruption risk?  

### b. Ban on Conflicting / Opaque Roles  
- No concurrent or recent (3–5 year cooling-off) positions in NGOs, think tanks, consultancies, or entities that lobby/receive public funds  
- Strict divestment & blind trusts required **[Debatable]** – definition of “lobbying entity”? Impact on civil society?  

### c. Independent Oversight via Sortition  
- Oversight panels drawn by **random lottery** from qualified experts/citizens (1-year mandatory service, with opt-outs for hardship)  
- Panels headed by **two proven experts** — one from each major ideological spectrum  
- Panels audit agencies, procurement, conflicts; have investigation + recommendation powers **[Debatable]** – how to define “qualified”? Panel size? Veto vs. recommend only?  

### d. Compensation Tied to Society  
- Representatives’ pay = fixed multiple of national **median** income (e.g. 1.5–2×)  
- No lavish pensions; standard social security only **[Debatable]** – multiple too low/high? Bonuses for good outcomes?  

### e. Criminal Deterrence  
- Proven corruption, bribery, embezzlement, influence-peddling, or clear ulterior motives → **mandatory minimum prison sentence** (5–10 years)  
- Lifetime ban from public office + asset forfeiture  
- Fast-track independent prosecution; no plea deals for political cases **[Debatable]** – sentence length? Definition of “ulterior motives”? Risk of weaponization?  

## 5. Transparency & Participation Safeguards  
- **All non-security communications** public by default (blockchain-style ledger encouraged) **[Debatable]** – chilling effect on candid discussion?  
- Optional **liquid democracy** elements (delegate votes via app)  
- **Sortition-based citizens’ assemblies** for major constitutional questions or vetoes  
- Strong federalism / subsidiarity – most decisions devolved to lowest effective level **[Debatable]** – how much central override?  

## 6. Economic & Fiscal Guardrails  
- Constitutional balanced-budget rule with independent fiscal council enforcement  
- Inflation devaluation triggers same ineligibility penalties as deficits  
- Strong antitrust + market freedom; no sectoral micromanagement **[Debatable]** – monetary policy independence? Target inflation rate?  

## Invitation to Collaborate  
This is **not** a finished constitution—it is a **starting sketch** meant to be torn apart, rebuilt, simulated, compared to real-world cases (Switzerland, Estonia, Singapore, ancient Athens), and stress-tested.  

**Ways to contribute**:
- Open issues with critiques / alternatives  
- Submit PRs editing any section (especially the **[Debatable]** points)  
- Add folders: `/simulations`, `/case-studies`, `/drafts`, `/critiques`  
- Propose better names (current favorite: Vigilant Integrity Republic)
